Will Smith showcases exceptional puck-handling abilities, employing deceptive moves and fakes to outmaneuver opponents effectively. Transitioning from a point-per-game U17 season to over two points per game in U18, he's now on track to surpass the 50-point milestone in his freshman college year. Beyond his natural skill, Smith impresses with his cerebral play style and adeptness at slot passing, often drawing attention and creating opportunities for his teammates. His versatility allows him to excel in various aspects of the game, including passing, shooting, and deking, making him a formidable presence on the ice. While he still has areas for improvement, particularly in physical engagement and defensive play, his development trajectory suggests he'll become a key player in the NHL, likely filling a top-six center role and contributing significantly on the power play.

Musty, the top pick in the 2021 OHL Priority Selection, showcased impressive offensive prowess for the Wolves despite a brief setback due to a hand injury. Standing at 6-foot-2 and over 200 pounds, he possesses both strength and athleticism, complemented by slick puckhandling skills and a balanced skating stride. His versatility allows him to excel in various aspects of the game, from heavy shots to adept passing and creating plays along the wall. While his raw potential and production have drawn attention, questions about his competitiveness and decision-making have surfaced in the past, although recent reports suggest improvement in these areas. With continued development, Musty has the potential to evolve into a valuable second-line winger in the NHL, given his promising skill set and room for growth.

Bystedt possesses impressive size and mobility, particularly noticeable when he gains momentum through the neutral zone. He excels in puck control, utilizing both physicality and finesse to protect the puck and maneuver around opponents. While his agility may be limited in tight spaces, his strong north-south skating and wide crossover paths enable effective movement on the ice. Standing at 6-foot-3 and nearly 200 pounds, his reach and physical presence make him a formidable force in puck protection, allowing him to thrive in the SHL as a teenager. Over the past two years, he has shown growth in consistency and versatility, becoming a reliable contributor on both ends of the ice. With his combination of strength, skill, and improved game awareness, he projects as a valuable asset as a third-line center at the professional level.
